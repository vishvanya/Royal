Brunch is an essential part of the Seattle weekend: Its a hangover cure, a pick-me-up, and the best way to fuel up for a busy Saturday or Sunday. Given that this meal is perpetually oh-so-hot, Eater presents the Seattle Brunch Heatmap, collecting the restaurants opened within the past six months or so with outstanding midday meals, as well as older establishments that have newly revamped their brunch game to great effect.

Added this month: Revel and Sisters and Brothers at Sneaky Tiki.

Note: Restaurants on this map are listed geographically.

1. Eight Row

Copy Link
7102 Woodlawn Ave NE
Seattle, WA 98115
(206) 294-3178
(206) 294-3178
Visit Website

Green Lakes promising new farm-to-table restaurant, Eight Row, showcases the ingredients, influences, and aesthetics from the Wenatchee, WA, orchards that chef David Nicholss family owns and operates. Brunch runs weekends from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. with dishes like polenta with hominy, a fried egg, and salsa verde; crispy sweetbreads and waffles with spiced honey; and Dungeness crab and avocado salad with barnaise sauce.

2. Revel

Copy Link
401 N 36th St
Seattle, WA 98103
(206) 547-2040
(206) 547-2040
Visit Website

Long-running Korean staple Revel has finally returned to its Fremont home, which now sits in a brand new building. Celebrated chefs Rachel Yang and Seif Chirchi whip up dishes like the short rib patty melt, monkey bread with Spam and spicy maple syrup, and ramen with kimchi and bacon bits. Its one more fantastic take on the modern, lively approach thats made this spot beloved for years.

Copy Link
945 Elliott Ave W #201

The gussied-up homage to classic diner fare at Interbays new Champagne Diner comes from the ace team at Pioneer Squares always outstanding Damn the Weather. 4. Ba Sa Restaurant

Copy Link
101 Winslow Way E
Bainbridge Island, WA 98110
(206) 565-3287
(206) 565-3287
Visit Website

Bainbridge Island has gained a veritable brunch destination in Ba Sa, the new Vietnamese restaurant set in downtown Winslow. Here, owners Trinh and Thai Nguyen get crafty with the flavors of their Southeast Asian childhood in dishes like chicken and taro bubble waffles, passionfruit butter French toast, and banh bot chien (an egg dish, with clams, papaya, and chili soy sauce). Cocktails take a similar tack, with options like the soursop martini with Iichiko Silhouette sochu and a bone broth bloody mary.

5. Olmstead

Copy Link
314 Broadway E
Seattle, WA 98102
Visit Website

The lights are finally on inside Capitol Hills long-empty Broadway Grill space thanks to Olmstead. The menu features well-done takes on American classics and diner fare, with Dungeness crab deviled eggs, marmalade bread pudding with blood orange anglaise, and housemade pastrami hash with mirepoix. Brunch gets even livelier with television screens showing sports, and shuffleboard, and a long list of booze.

6. No Anchor

Copy Link
2505 2nd Ave #105
Seattle, WA 98121
(206) 448-2610
(206) 448-2610
Visit Website

Belltowns cozy No Anchor puts an inventive spin on the beer bar concept, with beer cocktails, an array of offbeat brews, and top-notch bites. The James Beard Award-nominated restaurant is flexing its brunch muscles with an expanded menu of duck confit hash, wagyu buger, and a bloody mary with smoked and pickled accompaniments. Its served Fridays, as well as weekends, from 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. (reservations here on Tock, not Open Table).

7. Musang

Copy Link
2524 Beacon Ave S
Seattle, WA 98144
(206) 708-6871
(206) 708-6871
Visit Website

Beacon Hills new Filipino restaurant is a veritable dining destination, and brunch is no exception. Served weekends from 10:30 a.m. to 2 p.m., the meal involves four rotating courses for $35. Diners might start with roasted mushroom lugaw, a rice porridge, then move to corned beef on garlic rice with a fried egg and laing, a coconut milk- ad taro-based dish with seasonal vegetables. There are vegetarian options available, too.
